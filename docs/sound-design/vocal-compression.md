# Vocal compression chain

Use serial compression: first a fast compressor for peaks, then a slower compressor for body.
Start with the fast stage around 2:1 to 4:1 ratio, medium-fast attack, and fast release.

For the leveling stage, use slower attack to preserve consonant clarity and musical transients.
Aim for consistent gain reduction rather than heavy pumping.

If vocals sound dull after compression, add parallel air with an EQ shelf after the compressors.
If they sound harsh, reduce high-mid buildup before adding brightness.

A de-esser usually sits before or between compressor stages when sibilance triggers too much
gain reduction. Tune the de-esser frequency to vocal-specific "s" energy, then rebalance makeup gain.
